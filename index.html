Out of the Fire

  • High Concept: As a piece of sentient coal, your best friend and greatest enemy helps you solve puzzles while you try not to get burnt.
  • Player Experience Goals: A love hate realationship with their main tool for solving puzzles which also happens to be their only enemey.
  • Genre: 2D PUzzle Platformer
  • Game play: The player controls a single piece of coal that can only jump around the environment. The player is constantly followed by a tiny flame that will hurt them if they touch it, but can be used to solve environmental puzzles. Puzzles involve lighting lamps or candles with the fire to open doors or new parts of the environment. Candles don't stay lit forever, and some lamps require a specific order to be lit in. The player must learn to fling the tiny flame in order for it to reach places the player couldn't reach. The flame ignores all obsticals except water. Water can cool off the player to keep them from dying in more difficult portions of the game, but can also douse the fire requiring the player to return to the source of the fire to regain the tiny flame that must be used to advance. The fire can be used to propel the player to higher ledges hurting the player in the process. After each contact with the flame, the coal gets a little redder each time, after the third hit, they die and must start over from the beginning. Throughout the game the moon slowly wanes in the sky until the world is pitch black, resulting in more difficult settings where the tiny flame and the torhes lit on the way are the only source of light.
  • Prototype goal: The protype is evaluating the mechanic of using a persistant enemy as a puzzle solving tool.
  • Features: The flinging mechanic and the enemey / tool relationship of the fire.
  • Setting/Premise: A dark world full of mystery. It is purpetually night, and the only way to move forward is to light one's way. There are lamps and candles everywhere and so many different types since they are the only source of light in this world besides the ever waining moon. It is a time before electricity and everything is powered by coal.
  • Story: The plot revolves around a sentient piece of coal that wakes up one day and walks out of the fire, but he is the fire's only friend so the fire too gets up and follows his friend to whereever their journey takes them. The piece of coal's goal is to excape the fire and discover what else the world has to offer.
  • Target Audience: Young children through young adults who prefer cute wimsical protagonists but crave a darker setting.
  • Play time: Currently less than a minute, but planned to be between 1 and 2 hours.
  • Strategies: Flinging the fire to reach sealed off locations and burning themself to reach distant ledges.
